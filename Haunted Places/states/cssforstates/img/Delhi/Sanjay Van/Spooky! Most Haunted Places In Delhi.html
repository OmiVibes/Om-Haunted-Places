Spooky! Most Haunted Places In Delhi

By Harshit Mansukhani

Image Credit @ iStock

Haunting Events In Delhi

We all thought ghosts were just fiction pieces of writing for shows, movies and dramas but what if we were wrong the whole time? Here’s a collection of places in Delhi that are not only spooky but also dangerous. 

Video by Life-Of-Vids from Pixabay

Delhi Cantonment

Delhi Cantonment has been touted as one of the safest areas in the National Capital but things go south as soon as the sun sets. Many late-night commuters have reported a woman in a white saree running parallel to their cars and almost the same speed. 


Image Credit @ iStock

Lothian Cemetery

Ghosts scare us, but a beheaded ghost will almost leave us begging for life. Lothian Cemetry at Delhi’s Kashmere Gate is home to a beheaded ghost who holds his head in one hand and chases after people. That is spooky!


Image Credit @ iStock

Sanjay Van

One of the most beautiful parks in Delhi for exercise and routine walks but not a great place to visit once it is dark. An elderly lady in a white saree, an evil laugh of children who cannot be seen. Sudden disappearance, intentional push, and mysterious fog on a summer evening make it one of the scariest places in the country. 


Image Credit @ iStock

Agrasen Ki Baoli

Agrasen Ki Baoli is at the heart of Delhi’s heritage tourism but many people have reported scary noises after the sun sets. 

Image Credit @ iStock

Firoz Shah Kotla Fort

People have reported accounts of bad angels that can possess people. Many of the neighbourhood residents try to please the demonic spirits but many have witnessed daunting experiences. 


Image Credit @ iStock

Khooni Darwaza

Blood-curdling screams and anguished cries can be heard around this historical monument. Even the name of the Darwaza is scary.
